            About This Blog

            In this blog I talk about how this blog was built, developed, and transformed to the state we now see today.

            # Why Not Social Networks

            In recent years, less and less people bother about creating their personal websites as more and more people pursue Instagrm pictures and Twitter/Weibo posts w/ less than 140 characters.

            Simpler and easier, more dazzling and arousing, pictures and funny videos. They sell.

            After my brother KJ told me that Chinese interact their friends through WeChat moments w/ no more than 9 pictures and a like click, I once seriously doubt the value of creating this site. But I made up my mind to continue this site after reading this popular essay, and I quote:

            It’s a place where we can express, on our terms, who we are and what we offer.

            So I own a small part of the Internet now 😁

            # Built in Jekyll

            In early 2018, I used Jekyll as the static site generator to construct a MVP.Gradually, I found it hard to extend, such as multi-language support, table of contents on sidebar and dark mode. It's not impossible to add an essential feature, but I feel I have to get my hands quite dirty (see Docker doc). I had a list of stuff I want my blog to have but hard to achieve in reasonable amount of time if keep using Jekyll.

            # Transformed to VuePress

            In early 2020, while searching for alternatives, I was quite excited to discover *VuePress*, which is based on Vue.js, a framework I've always been quite curious about, after having learned some Angular.js and React.js. Using VuePress, bloggers can build a SPA (blazing fast w/i the site) w/ dynamic Vue components, rather than a static site compiled by Jekyll.

            My passion faded a bit due to unfamiliarity about Vue.js and VuePress, until I searched "VuePress blog theme" on GitHub and found the theme reco. I marvelled at its simplicity and elegance. IT HAS ALMOST ALL THE FEATURES I NEED, if not more! So I spent a few days to make some changes most in structure and few in blog posts to move my old blogs from Jekyll to vuepress-theme-reco.
